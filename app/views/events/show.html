Accomodations

We picked a very popular weekend to get married -- not only is it our wedding weekend, but it’s Cal’s freshman move in weekend too! Unfortunately, that means that hotel prices in the area are higher than normal. Air BnB or VRBO are likely your best bets in finding reasonably priced accommodations. Please book sooner rather than later -- they fill up quickly! If you’re relying on public transportation to get around, stick to rentals in Berkeley or Oakland. If you have access to a car, rentals in Berkeley, Albany, Emeryville, Oakland, El Cerrito, or Crockett itself should work wonderfully. Here are some options:

Directions:

The Old Homestead - Crocket, CA >

Google Maps gives you a bit of a round about way to get to the Old Homestead. These directions are easier, but both will get you there!

    From San Francisco/East Bay
  • Take I-80 East toward Sacramento
  • Take exit 27 for Pomona St toward Crockett (the last exit before the Carquinez Bridge)
  • Turn left off the freeway onto Pomona St.
  • Follow Pomona street through town, past John Swett High School (on the left)
  • After the high school, turn left on Rolph Ave.
  • Rolph Ave wlll dead-end into Loring Ave. Turn left.
  • The parking lot for the Old Homestead will be on your left on that same block. The driveway is just past the Crockett Library.
  • The Old Homestead is in the back right corner of the parking lot. Park wherever you'd like!
    From Sacramento
  • Take I-80 East toward Sacramento
  • Take exit 27 for Pomona St toward Crockett(the first exit after the Carquinez Bridge)
  • Turn left off the freeway onto Pomona St.
  • Follow Pomona street through town, past John Swett High School (on the left)

Dress Code

Casual -- wear something you feel good in! The ceremony and reception will both be outside, and while it'll be hot during the day, it can cool down at night. So, please bring layers! Shoes that you feel comfortable walking on grass and gravel in are a good idea, too.

Potluck

“When you invite someone to sit at your table and you want to cook for them, you're inviting a person into your life”

- Maya Angelou

Our favorite meals have always been those we’ve shared in the homes of our family and friends. To celebrate our marriage and the gathering of our community, we want to recreate that type of meal with all of you. To that end, we’re hosting a wedding potluck! You’re invited to participate by bringing a dish that’s meaningful to you -- an old family recipe, something that you love to eat, or something that you’re excited to make and share with others. We’ll provide a main dish, cake, and beverages. We can’t wait to celebrate with all of you and share a meal that’s full of history, made with love, and thoughtfully created by the people we care about the most. Participating in the potluck is entirely up to you -- if you’re unable or don’t want to participate, that is perfectly okay. Either way, we’re so excited to share this day with you!
